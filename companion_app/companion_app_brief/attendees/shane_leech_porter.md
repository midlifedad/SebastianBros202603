## Detailed Profile

Shane Leech-Porter is a Victoria, British Columbia–based executive and CFA charterholder who became CEO of Kurnl in September 2025, leading the launch of a Canadian open-access fibre company focused on multi-family buildings ([LinkedIn profile](https://www.linkedin.com/in/sl-p)).

At Kurnl, his stated focus is getting the “fundamentals right” (operations, financials, positioning, partner development) while building relationships with developers and internet service providers (ISPs) and bringing a transparent, developer-first proposition to market ([LinkedIn post on early Kurnl progress](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/sl-p_realestatedevelopment-digitalinfrastructure-activity-7424106976009244672-lNuM)).

Kurnl positions itself as a turnkey designer/builder/operator of open-access fibre networks for multi-family buildings, aiming to create building revenue streams, enable neutral ISP competition, and provide residents “day-zero” connectivity; it also states BC and Alberta networks opening in 2026 and provides early network stats (active units, fibre-installed units, uptime, provider count) ([Kurnl website](https://www.kurnl.ca)).

Before Kurnl, Leech-Porter spent ~15 years at British Columbia Investment Management Corporation (BCI/bcIMC) in public markets/thematic investing leadership roles, including Managing Director, Global Thematic (2018–2025), and a period as Interim Senior Managing Director, Active Internal Equities (Aug 2021–Apr 2022) with responsibility for seven equity teams and ~C$20B AUM ([LinkedIn profile](https://www.linkedin.com/in/sl-p)).

In his BCI experience descriptions, he reports building and leading a seven-person Global Thematic team; chairing a multi-asset thematic advisory committee; serving on a senior investment advisory committee; and growing the Global Thematic program to a peak of ~$6.5B AUM with multiple internal clients adopting the program ([LinkedIn profile](https://www.linkedin.com/in/sl-p)).

Earlier career roles include equity research associate positions at TD Securities (2008–2010) and Desjardins Securities (2006–2008), investment banking analyst at BMO Capital Markets (2004–2005), and a finance internship at Terasen Inc. (summer 2003) where he managed large cash accounts via money market transactions ([LinkedIn profile](https://www.linkedin.com/in/sl-p)).

Education and credentials: Bachelor of Commerce (Honours), Finance, University of British Columbia (1999–2003) and CFA designation earned in the mid-2000s (as reflected in third-party aggregations and his profile name) ([ContactOut profile](https://contactout.com/Shane-Leech-porter-135401859); [LinkedIn profile](https://www.linkedin.com/in/sl-p)).

## How AI & Exponential Technology Impacts Their World

For an open-access fibre CEO, AI’s biggest operational impact is shifting network operations from manual/threshold-based monitoring to data-driven, increasingly autonomous operations (anomaly detection, root-cause reasoning, and self-healing remediation) to protect uptime while keeping operating cost low as footprint scales ([McKinsey on AI-driven telecom networks](https://www.mckinsey.com.br/industries/technology-media-and-telecommunications/our-insights/issue-brief-ai-driven-telecom-networks)).

Because Kurnl’s model depends on high availability and low-friction ISP onboarding across many buildings, AI-enabled service assurance (detecting emerging issues before residents notice) becomes a differentiator that supports both resident experience and ISP partner satisfaction ([Innovation, Science and Economic Development Canada principles for AI adoption in telecom](https://ised-isde.canada.ca/site/ised/en/principles-artificial-intelligence-adoption-telecommunications-industry)).

AI can also directly improve field operations by forecasting faults (predictive maintenance) and optimizing technician dispatch windows, reducing emergency truck rolls and increasing the reliability metrics Kurnl markets to developers and owners ([Turn-Key Technologies on AI predictive maintenance](https://www.turn-keytechnologies.com/blog/ai-in-predictive-maintenance-for-network-systems)).

Commercially, AI changes demand dynamics: hyperscale AI and data-center buildouts are increasing fiber intensity and placing a premium on scalable fibre supply chains and AI-ready connectivity architectures; that macro tailwind is relevant for strategic positioning, partnerships, and long-term capex planning even for a building-focused open-access operator ([Fiber Broadband Association AI + fiber white paper](https://fiberbroadband.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/FBA-087_AI_WhitePaper_FIN.pdf)).

Key challenges AI can address for his role: (1) scaling operations without linear headcount growth (AIOps, automated ticket triage, automated change-risk analysis), (2) compressing time-to-serve (faster proposals, standardized designs, faster ISP onboarding), (3) proving reliability and ROI to developers with credible, explainable metrics, and (4) governance—deploying AI in ways that are safe, secure, and trustworthy (privacy, security, and IP respect), which is explicitly emphasized in Canadian telecom AI-adoption guidance ([Innovation, Science and Economic Development Canada principles for AI adoption in telecom](https://ised-isde.canada.ca/site/ised/en/principles-artificial-intelligence-adoption-telecommunications-industry)).

## Personalized AI Resource Guide

### AI Tools Recommended

1) **AIOps / Service assurance copilots**: AI assistants that correlate alerts, logs, tickets, and topology to accelerate root-cause analysis and remediation—useful for maintaining Kurnl’s uptime promise as more buildings come online (e.g., “smart co-pilots and root-cause models” described in telecom issue-management journeys) ([McKinsey on AI-driven telecom networks](https://www.mckinsey.com.br/industries/technology-media-and-communications/our-insights/issue-brief-ai-driven-telecom-networks)).

2) **Predictive maintenance analytics**: ML systems that forecast equipment or link degradation from operational telemetry so maintenance can be scheduled proactively, reducing downtime and emergency repairs in distributed networks ([Turn-Key Technologies on AI predictive maintenance](https://www.turn-keytechnologies.com/blog/ai-in-predictive-maintenance-for-network-systems)).

3) **AI-assisted proposal + contracting workflows**: GenAI drafting tools (for RFP responses, builder/developer proposals, ISP/MSA drafts, technical appendices) to shorten cycles; the value is highest where Kurnl is repeatedly customizing a core proposition for different developers and buildings (grounded in his LinkedIn-described focus on partner engagement and proposals) ([LinkedIn post on Vancouver developer meetings](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/sl-p_realestatedevelopment-multifamily-rentalhousing-activity-7427004095711952897-4cyQ)).

4) **Customer/support automation with strong privacy controls**: GenAI support assistants for installation day-zero issues, resident onboarding, and ISP coordination—aligned with telecom emphasis on improving customer experience while adopting AI safely and securely ([Innovation, Science and Economic Development Canada principles for AI adoption in telecom](https://ised-isde.canada.ca/site/ised/en/principles-artificial-intelligence-adoption-telecommunications-industry)).

5) **Knowledge management + engineering search**: Internal “network bible” Q&A that ingests as-builts, standard designs, ISP integration runbooks, and past incidents—directly accelerates onboarding and reduces single points of failure (supported by the broader trend toward AI copilots for issue management) ([McKinsey on AI-driven telecom networks](https://www.mckinsey.com.br/industries/technology-media-and-telecommunications/our-insights/issue-brief-ai-driven-telecom-networks)).

### Agentic Frameworks & Apps

1) **AIOps agents for incident lifecycle automation**: Multi-step agents that (a) detect anomalies, (b) gather context (topology, recent changes), (c) propose remediation, and (d) open/route tickets to the right human with a ready-to-execute runbook—mirroring the AI-enabled “issue management journey” described for telcos ([McKinsey on AI-driven telecom networks](https://www.mckinsey.com.br/industries/technology-media-and-telecommunications/our-insights/issue-brief-ai-driven-telecom-networks)).

2) **Change-risk + rollout agents**: Agents that review planned network changes (firmware, config, ISP integration) for disruption risk and propose rollback/mitigation plans, consistent with “critical change agents” described in telecom AI operations ([McKinsey on AI-driven telecom networks](https://www.mckinsey.com.br/industries/technology-media-and-telecommunications/our-insights/issue-brief-ai-driven-telecom-networks)).

3) **RevOps / partnership pipeline agents**: Agents that maintain a structured CRM view of developer and ISP accounts, summarize last interactions, draft follow-ups, and generate meeting briefs—high leverage given his current focus on partner development and proposals ([LinkedIn post on Vancouver developer meetings](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/sl-p_realestatedevelopment-multifamily-rentalhousing-activity-7427004095711952897-4cyQ)).

4) **Design-to-deploy workflow automation**: Agentic checklists for each building deployment (design review → permitting artifacts → construction coordination → ISP onboarding → resident launch) to reduce variance across projects, aligned with Kurnl’s positioning as turnkey delivery ([Kurnl website](https://www.kurnl.ca)).

### Key Aspects of Their Role Being Transformed by AI

- **Network reliability management**: Moving from reactive incident response to predictive detection and partially automated remediation to sustain very high uptime while scaling footprint ([Turn-Key Technologies on AI predictive maintenance](https://www.turn-keytechnologies.com/blog/ai-in-predictive-maintenance-for-network-systems); [McKinsey on AI-driven telecom networks](https://www.mckinsey.com.br/industries/technology-media-and-telecommunications/our-insights/issue-brief-ai-driven-telecom-networks)).

- **Partner enablement and proposals**: Faster creation of developer proposals and clearer articulation of building-level ROI and value proposition—directly relevant to the work he describes doing with multi-family developers and the invitations to submit proposals ([LinkedIn post on Vancouver developer meetings](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/sl-p_realestatedevelopment-multifamily-rentalhousing-activity-7427004095711952897-4cyQ)).

- **Operational scaling**: Standardizing and automating the recurring “building launch” process (design, installation coordination, ISP onboarding, resident onboarding) so growth does not require linear headcount increases ([Kurnl website](https://www.kurnl.ca)).

- **Security and trust**: More sophisticated anomaly detection for security/fraud, plus governance practices to deploy AI in ways that are safe and trustworthy—an explicit industry emphasis in Canada’s telecom AI-adoption principles ([Innovation, Science and Economic Development Canada principles for AI adoption in telecom](https://ised-isde.canada.ca/site/ised/en/principles-artificial-intelligence-adoption-telecommunications-industry)).

- **AI-native telecom operations (issue management, self-healing)**: Operators are using AI for advanced anomaly detection, root-cause reasoning copilots, and automated remediation and ticket routing to reduce outage duration and operating cost ([McKinsey on AI-driven telecom networks](https://www.mckinsey.com.br/industries/technology-media-and-telecommunications/our-insights/issue-brief-ai-driven-telecom-networks)).
